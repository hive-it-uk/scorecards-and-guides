Build your own personalised plan

Since you already use AI regularly, a generic course list won't tell you much you don't already know. Instead, this prompt — pasted into Claude or Gemini — will ask you about your actual habits before making any recommendations. The plan you get back will be specific to you, not to your job title.

How to use it

  1. Copy the prompt below into Claude, Gemini, or whichever AI assistant you use most.
  2. Fill in your role where it says [FILL IN].
  3. Answer the questions honestly. The plan is only as useful as your answers.
  4. Save the plan you get back, and revisit it every few months.

The prompt

Prompt — copy and paste into your AI assistant 
I want you to help me build a personalised AI training plan. I already use AI tools in my work, so don't start from absolute basics — figure out where I actually am first.

My role at Hive IT is: [FILL IN — e.g. UX designer, backend developer, bid writer, delivery manager, account manager]

Before recommending anything, ask me about:
1. Which AI tools I currently use (e.g. Claude, Gemini, GitHub Copilot, JetBrains AI) and how often
2. What I actually use them for day to day — be specific, not just "writing" or "coding"
3. Where I get stuck, get poor results, or avoid using AI because it's not worth the effort
4. Whether I've done any formal AI training before, and what it covered
5. How much time I can realistically commit per month (be honest — an unrealistic plan won't get followed)
6. My budget, if I'm paying for this myself, or whether it's coming from Hive's training budget (currently £700 per person per year)

Ask me these one at a time or in a short batch, not all at once with no room to explain.

Once you have my answers, give me:
- A short, honest assessment of my current skill level (don't inflate it to be kind)
- 2–4 specific courses or resources, named directly with links where you can find them, in the order I should do them
- A reason for each one tied to something I actually told you, not a generic justification
- An estimate of total time and cost
- One thing to skip, if something I mentioned suggests I don't need a course everyone else might

Keep the whole thing short enough to act on. I don't want a long report — I want a plan I can start this week.

Use British English and plain language throughout, in line with GOV.UK writing guidelines. Where you recommend a paid course, always check whether a free alternative covers the same ground before recommending the paid one. Cite sources for anything you state as fact about a course (price, length, certificate) — don't guess.

Why not just a course list?

Once you're already using AI regularly, your gaps are individual. One developer might be fluent in Copilot but never use AI for documentation. One bid writer might prompt well but skip ethical disclosure steps entirely. A list matched to your job title can't catch that. This prompt makes the AI ask before it recommends, so the plan it gives you is based on what you actually told it — not on a generic assumption.

A note on honesty

The prompt asks the AI not to inflate your skill assessment. A plan built on an honest starting point is the only kind that actually closes a skills gap. If you'd rather get a flattering result, this isn't the right tool.

Process note for bid writing

Training alone will not fix bid efficiency. AI works best when the team has a governed knowledge base of approved case studies, policies, and past responses. Building that library should happen alongside training, not after it.
