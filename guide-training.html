Bid writing

nFold: APMP AI Micro-Certification

  • Cost: Approximately £295 + VAT
  • Certificate: Yes — APMP AI Micro-Certification, industry-recognised
  • Format: Online self-paced modules followed by a live session
  • Link: nfold.com/ai-micro-certification-masterclass

Covers the complete bid lifecycle — opportunity qualification, RFP analysis, proposal writing, compliance review, and post-bid learning. Explicitly addresses confidentiality, compliance, and data governance. APMP credentials are recognised by public sector procurement teams.

Process note for bid writing

Training alone will not fix bid efficiency. AI works best when the team has a governed knowledge base of approved case studies, policies, and past responses. Building that library should happen alongside training, not after it.

Since you already use AI tools regularly, the nFold course's advanced modules on compliance review and post-bid learning will likely be more useful to you than the earlier lifecycle modules — consider starting there and doubling back if needed.

Claude-specific training Added for Claude users

Hive IT has a Claude Teams subscription, so getting more out of Claude is a direct return on a tool you already pay for. The three courses below are free — do them in order. The certification at the end is paid and optional.

Step 1 — AI Fluency: Framework and Foundations

  • Provider: Anthropic, with Prof. Rick Dakan (Ringling College) and Prof. Joseph Feller (University College Cork)
  • Cost: Free
  • Certificate: Yes — certificate of completion after a final assessment
  • Time: Approximately 3 hours
  • Link: anthropic.skilljar.com — AI Fluency: Framework and Foundations

Anthropic's own course on working with AI well, built around four competencies: delegation, description, discernment, and diligence. The diligence strand — being clear about when AI is used and taking ownership of what you produce — maps directly onto Hive's public sector transparency obligations.

Step 2 — Prompt Engineering for Claude

  • Provider: FreeAcademy
  • Cost: Free
  • Certificate: Yes — certificate of completion after a final exam
  • Link: freeacademy.ai — Prompt Engineering for Claude

Goes beyond generic prompting into Claude-specific techniques: XML tags, extended thinking, system prompt design, CLAUDE.md files, and MCP integration. Patterns apply whether you use the Claude app or the API.

Step 3 — Interactive Prompt Engineering Tutorial

  • Provider: Anthropic
  • Cost: Free
  • Certificate: No — a hands-on practice companion, not a course
  • Link: github.com/anthropics/prompt-eng-interactive-tutorial

Anthropic's official exercise set. Work through it alongside Step 2 rather than after it — the exercises give you somewhere to practise each technique as you learn it.

Optional — Claude Certified Architect: Foundations (CCA-F)

  • Provider: Anthropic
  • Cost: Free prep materials, plus a paid exam (USD $99 per attempt, roughly £80)
  • Certificate: Yes — Anthropic's first official technical certification, launched March 2026
  • Format: 60 scenario-based questions, 120 minutes, proctored
  • Link: Registration is through Anthropic's certification portal — see anthropic.com/learn for current access details

An architecture-level exam covering the Claude Agent SDK, Claude Code, and the Model Context Protocol. It's aimed at people building production systems with Claude, so it's most relevant to developers — Anthropic recommends at least 6 months of hands-on experience first. Access has so far been routed through Anthropic's partner network, so check current availability before budgeting for it.

Added cost: free for the three courses; approximately £80 if you sit the optional CCA-F exam.
